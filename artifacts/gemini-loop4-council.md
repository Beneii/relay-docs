# Loop 4 Council: Highest-Impact Work — Gemini Council

1. **Quiet Hours & Notification Preferences per Device/Channel:** This is the most impactful feature for converting Free users to Pro and reducing churn. For a developer building 24/7 autonomous agents or trading bots, the inability to silence non-critical notifications (e.g., "info" status updates) between 11 PM and 7 AM is a major pain point. By offering granular "Quiet Hours" and the ability to enable/disable specific channels, Relay moves from being a "noisy buzzer" to a sophisticated, professional-grade monitoring tool that fits into the user's life without causing alert fatigue.

2. **Team/Dashboard Sharing (Pro Feature):** This is the strongest "Network Effect" feature and a key justification for the $7.99/mo price point. Allowing developers to share a specific dashboard's notification stream with teammates (e.g., a devops alert sent to three people at once) makes Relay indispensable for small teams and home automation enthusiasts. It transitions the product from a "personal toy" to a "team infrastructure" tool, significantly increasing its stickiness and revenue potential.

3. **SDK Publication to npm & Webhook/API Key Management UI:** Publishing `@relayapp/sdk` to npm and providing a clear UI for managing named API keys (instead of just webhook tokens) is a major "Developer Experience" (DX) multiplier. For the vibe-coder, being able to simply `npm install @relayapp/sdk` and manage keys with descriptive names (e.g., "GitHub Actions Prod", "Local Cursor Agent") makes the integration feel like a first-class citizen in their stack. This also provides an opportunity to clearly display the webhook URL for non-SDK use cases, closing the final major documentation gap in the dashboard.
